About Silkworms

Silkworms (Bombyx mori) are domesticated insects reared for silk production. The art of cultivating silkworms is known as sericulture, a practice that originated in China over 5,000 years ago and spread across Asia and the world.

Silkworm Diseases & Recovery

Pebrine (Protozoan disease)

Symptoms: Irregular black spots on larvae, poor cocooning.

Prevention: Use disease-free eggs, disinfect rearing trays.

Treatment: Avoid rearing from infected moths, proper hygiene.

Grasserie (Viral disease)

Symptoms: Swollen larvae with shiny skin, white liquid oozes.

Prevention: Maintain low humidity, avoid overcrowding.

Treatment: Remove infected worms, spray 2% bleaching powder.

Flacherie (Bacterial disease)

Symptoms: Worms lose appetite, become soft and die suddenly.

Prevention: Maintain cleanliness, proper leaf feeding.

Medicines: Streptomycin solution in mulberry leaves.

Muscardine (Fungal disease)

Symptoms: Dead larvae covered in white fungal coating.

Prevention: Dry atmosphere, dust lime powder on trays.

Treatment: Use 2% lime solution to disinfect rearing houses.
